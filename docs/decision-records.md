# Decision Records

## Purpose

Decision records capture important engineering choices so future agents and humans understand why the project moved in a direction.

## When Required

Write a decision record when a change:

- Chooses between meaningful architecture options
- Adds or removes a dependency
- Changes storage, auth, deployment, or data flow
- Sets an AI quality policy
- Accepts a known tradeoff or risk

## Compare Options

For each serious option, record:

- What it is
- Benefits
- Costs
- Risks
- Operational impact
- Why it was accepted or rejected

## Capture Consequences

Every decision should state:

- What becomes easier
- What becomes harder
- What must be monitored
- What future work is implied

## Revisit Triggers

Define when the decision should be revisited, such as scale limits, cost changes, security findings, user feedback, latency regressions, or repeated maintenance pain.
